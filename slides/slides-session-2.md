**Session 2 — Building Welcoming Communities**  
**Facilitator:** Alberto Camarena  
**Presented at:** Global Hack Week 🌐 OSS
**Date:** 13/08/25 📅

---

## Objectives

* Understand what makes a community “welcoming” in open source
* Learn the key elements of inclusive communication and behavior
* Explore tools and practices to foster engagement and trust
* Identify barriers to participation and strategies to overcome them

---

## Why Community Matters

> "People don’t contribute to codebases, they contribute to communities."

* Open source thrives on collaboration and trust
* Healthy communities attract and retain contributors
* Welcoming environments reduce barriers for newcomers

---

## The Anatomy of a Welcoming Community

**Core traits:**

* Clear communication channels (Issues, Discussions, Chat)
* Code of Conduct and enforcement
* Inclusive language and documentation
* Recognizing and celebrating contributors

---

## Communication and Inclusion

* Use clear, inclusive language
* Assume good intentions, but address harm when it happens
* Make decisions transparently
* Provide context and empathy in written communication

---

## Tools that Support Community Health

* **GitHub Discussions** – for open conversation and Q&A
* **Issue templates** – guide contributors to provide the right info
* **Welcome bots** – automate greetings and guidance
* **CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md** – sets behavioral expectations

---

## Handling Conflict

* Conflicts happen — they don’t have to break trust
* Use private channels for sensitive discussions
* Involve moderators when necessary
* Keep conversations focused on impact, not individuals

---

## Non-code Contributions Matter

* Documentation improvements
* Translation and localization
* Event organization and mentorship
* Design, testing, or writing release notes

> A strong community values *all* forms of contribution.

---

## Maintainer Perspective

**Maintainers shape the culture.**

* Set the tone in issues and PRs
* Balance feedback with encouragement
* Recognize burnout and take breaks
* Encourage shared ownership and delegation

---

## Quick Reflection

Take 2 minutes:

* What are examples of welcoming behaviors you’ve experienced online?
* How can you apply them to open source?
